About

Audio Alchemist / Engineer / Producer

Music saved my life. Not metaphorically, literally. When I was at my lowest, I found myself alone with Tame Impala's The Slow Rush, Pink Floyd's Animals, and MGMT's Congratulations on repeat. Those albums became a lifeline. They showed me that sound could be more than entertainment. It could be medicine, transformation, or even a portal to something greater.

That's when everything changed. I started recording local bands and artists out of necessity, teaching myself every instrument I could get my hands on, obsessively learning the craft of production and mixing. What began as survival became purpose. I moved to Colorado, got formally trained on SSL consoles and a variety of professional studios, and realized that my mission wasn't just to make music. It was to help others access the same healing and connection that saved me.

As a young, neurodivergent artist, I've experienced firsthand how the industry can make you feel like you don't belong. That experience helped shape Glue Matrix. This isn't about fitting into the old systems or playing by their rules. It's about building something new. A space where artists of all backgrounds, identities, and experiences are not just welcomed but celebrated. We have the power to DIY this whole thing, to change lives, to prove that outliers are also the ones who can help create the future.

Glue Matrix is built on the belief that music is alchemy. It's the universal language, the force that connects us all. Whether I'm crafting psychedelic soundscapes drenched in reverb and delay, engineering dense electronic productions, or tracking raw emotional vocal performances, my approach stays the same: I want you to feel seen and heard. Your vision, your emotion, & your story is what matters most.

I love and have experience with all genres, and I specialize in psych pop, normal pop, indie, electronic, rock, R&B, and anything that lives in the lush, layered, atmospheric space. I know the technical side. The gear, the consoles, the physics of sound. I know when to follow, and when to break the rules. But more importantly, I understand what music does to people. I've been there. I know what it means to need a song to make sense of your life. That's what I bring to every project: technical precision with a soul.

I have been working professionally in the front range since 2021. I currently operate from my own home studio with a collection of high-end gear and microphones, as well as at Dog House Music Studios.

If you're looking for someone who gets it, someone who sees your vision and has the skills to bring it to life, then let's work together.
